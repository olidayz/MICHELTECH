# Style Mixer

## What It Does
Blends two or more visual styles to create something unique. AI generation's superpower — you can combine styles that would be impossible or prohibitively expensive in traditional production.

## How to Mix

### The Process
1. **Pick Style A** — from any category (movement, format, era, animation, game, art, director, texture)
2. **Pick Style B** — from any category (the more unexpected the pairing, the more interesting)
3. **Identify the overlap** — what do they share? (mood, color, geometry, texture)
4. **Identify the tension** — what conflicts? This is where the interesting stuff lives.
5. **Describe the blend** — what does the baby look like?
6. **Generate a preview** — use nanobanana/Nano Banana Pro for quick in-chat examples
7. **Pull references** — search for closest existing work
8. **Write the prompt recipe** — how to achieve this in your generation tool

### Mixing Categories

**Same-category mixes** (safe, predictable):
- Neo-noir + Cyberpunk = pretty standard dark sci-fi
- Art Deco + Bauhaus = geometric modernism

**Cross-category mixes** (interesting):
- Film Noir + Anime = cel-shaded detective shadows
- VHS + Solarpunk = retro-recorded utopia, lo-fi hope
- Wes Anderson + Horror = symmetrical dread in pastels

**Triple mixes** (dangerous, potentially brilliant):
- Ukiyo-e + Cyberpunk + Tilt-Shift = woodblock neon miniature city
- Ghibli + CCTV + Brutalism = surveilled magical concrete worlds

## Danger Zones

Some combos fight each other — not always bad, but know the risk:

| Combo | Why it's tricky |
|-------|----------------|
| Hyperrealism + Heavy Grain | Grain destroys the detail that makes hyperrealism work |
| Minimalism + Baroque | Fundamentally opposite philosophies — can feel confused |
| IMAX + Lo-Fi | Scale vs intimacy — pick one as dominant |
| Multiple competing color palettes | Teal-orange + vaporwave pastels = muddy |

**The fix:** When styles conflict, make one DOMINANT and one ACCENT. Don't 50/50 opposing styles.

## Suggesting Mixes

When the user describes a project or mood, proactively suggest 2-3 unexpected combos:

1. Read the emotional map / story tone
2. Match to styles that serve the emotion
3. Find an unexpected second style that creates tension or surprise
4. Describe what the combo looks/feels like
5. Offer to generate preview images

**Always explain WHY the combo works** — connect it to the story/mood, don't just be random.

| Film/cinematic | Flim.ai, ShotDeck, Shot.cafe |
| Animation | Sakugabooru, ArtStation |
| Game aesthetics | ArtStation, Behance, itch.io |
| Art movements | WikiArt, Google Arts & Culture |
| Mixed/experimental | Behance, Vimeo, Pinterest |
| AI-generated examples | Civitai, Midjourney showcase |
| Music videos | FrameSet, IMVDb, YouTube |
| Commercials | Ads of the World, shots.net |

Present 3-5 references with links when exploring a style direction.

## The "Surprise Me" Mode

Pick two unlikely styles from different categories at random. Generate a preview. Describe it. Sometimes the best creative directions come from accidents.
