# Cinematic Movements & Styles

Each entry: what it looks like, when to use it, how to prompt it, reference films.

---

## Film Noir (1940s-1950s)
- **Look:** High contrast B&W, deep shadows, venetian blind patterns, rain-slicked streets, smoke, low-key lighting
- **Mood:** Moral ambiguity, fatalism, paranoia, desire
- **Use when:** Dark stories, detective/mystery, moral corruption, retrospective narration
- **Prompt language:** "film noir, high contrast black and white, dramatic shadows, venetian blind light, smoke-filled room, 1940s"
- **Reference:** Double Indemnity, The Third Man, Sunset Boulevard

## Neo-Noir
- **Look:** Noir sensibility in color. Neon against dark, saturated highlights in shadow, urban night
- **Mood:** Same moral ambiguity but modern, stylized, hypnotic
- **Use when:** Modern crime, psychological thriller, stylized urban stories
- **Prompt language:** "neo-noir, neon-lit darkness, rain reflections, high contrast color, urban night, saturated highlights"
- **Reference:** Drive, Blade Runner, Collateral, Nightcrawler, Sin City

## French New Wave (1960s)
- **Look:** Handheld camera, natural light, jump cuts, location shooting, breaking fourth wall, imperfect
- **Mood:** Freedom, rebellion, intellectual playfulness, youth
- **Use when:** Breaking conventions, romantic/philosophical stories, youth culture, raw authenticity
- **Prompt language:** "French New Wave, handheld, natural light, grainy black and white or muted color, Parisian streets, candid"
- **Reference:** Breathless, The 400 Blows, Cléo from 5 to 7

## Italian Neorealism (1940s-1950s)
- **Look:** Location shooting (not studios), non-professional actors, documentary feel, unglamorous
- **Mood:** Social reality, poverty, dignity in struggle, post-war truth
- **Use when:** Social commentary, realistic human stories, stripped-down authenticity
- **Prompt language:** "Italian neorealism, documentary style, natural light, location shooting, unglamorous, raw, post-war"
- **Reference:** Bicycle Thieves, Rome Open City, Umberto D.

## German Expressionism (1920s)
- **Look:** Extreme angles, distorted sets, stark shadows, exaggerated lighting, surreal architecture
- **Mood:** Psychological terror, madness, inner turmoil externalized
- **Use when:** Horror, psychological drama, nightmare sequences, surreal moments
- **Prompt language:** "German Expressionism, distorted architecture, extreme shadows, angular, stark lighting, surreal, Cabinet of Dr. Caligari"
- **Reference:** The Cabinet of Dr. Caligari, Nosferatu, Metropolis

## Soviet Montage (1920s)
- **Look:** Rapid editing, juxtaposition of images, graphic compositions, propaganda aesthetics
- **Mood:** Power, ideology, collective emotion, intellectual stimulation
- **Use when:** Montage sequences, propaganda-style content, intellectual cinema, graphic impact
- **Prompt language:** "Soviet montage, bold graphic composition, constructivist, high contrast, dynamic angles"
- **Reference:** Battleship Potemkin, Man with a Movie Camera

## Dogme 95
- **Look:** Handheld DV camera, natural light only, no music (unless diegetic), no props brought in, no genre
- **Mood:** Raw truth, stripping away artifice, confrontational intimacy
- **Use when:** Maximum authenticity, stripped-down drama, anti-Hollywood aesthetic
- **Prompt language:** "Dogme 95, handheld DV camera, natural light, no color grading, raw, unpolished, intimate"
- **Reference:** The Celebration, The Idiots, Italian for Beginners

## Mumblecore
- **Look:** Low budget, naturalistic dialogue (often improvised), digital camera, intimate settings
- **Mood:** Awkward realism, millennial anxiety, relatable discomfort
- **Use when:** Slice of life, relationship stories, comedy of discomfort
- **Prompt language:** "mumblecore, low-fi digital, natural light, apartment interior, casual, intimate, unpolished"
- **Reference:** Frances Ha, Drinking Buddies, The Puffy Chair

## Giallo (1970s Italian)
- **Look:** Saturated color (especially red), stylized violence, elaborate set pieces, POV killer shots
- **Mood:** Sensual dread, baroque horror, mystery
- **Use when:** Stylized horror/thriller, baroque visuals, murder mystery with visual flair
- **Prompt language:** "giallo, saturated reds, baroque lighting, 1970s Italian, stylized, ornate interiors, dramatic color"
- **Reference:** Suspiria (1977), Deep Red, The Bird with the Crystal Plumage

## Cyberpunk
- **Look:** Neon-drenched urban sprawl, rain, holographic ads, tech-grit contrast, night, wires, screens everywhere
- **Mood:** Dystopian, tech-saturated, corporate oppression, underground resistance
- **Use when:** Sci-fi, tech commentary, future urban stories, high-tech low-life
- **Prompt language:** "cyberpunk, neon cityscape, rain, holographic advertisements, dark alley, tech noir, dense urban, night"
- **Reference:** Blade Runner, Ghost in the Shell, Akira, The Matrix

## Solarpunk
- **Look:** Green technology, organic architecture, bright/hopeful palette, nature integrated with tech
- **Mood:** Optimistic future, sustainability, community, harmony
- **Use when:** Hopeful sci-fi, environmental themes, utopian vision, contrast to cyberpunk
- **Prompt language:** "solarpunk, green architecture, solar panels, lush vegetation integrated with modern design, bright, optimistic, sustainable city"
- **Reference:** Limited film examples — more art movement. Wakanda in Black Panther has elements.

## Afrofuturism
- **Look:** African cultural elements fused with sci-fi, bold patterns, futuristic technology, regal aesthetics
- **Mood:** Empowerment, cultural pride, reimagined futures, ancestral connection
- **Use when:** Sci-fi with African/diaspora cultural foundation, reimagining futures, cultural celebration
- **Prompt language:** "Afrofuturist, African cultural patterns, futuristic technology, regal, bold colors, cosmic, ancestral meets future"
- **Reference:** Black Panther, Neptune Frost, Janelle Monáe's Dirty Computer
